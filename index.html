Hi. I'm Tim. I am just about to graduate from the iO Academy Full Stack Track bootcamp and am now looking for my first role in software development.

Let me introduce myself.

About me

My journey to here....

Previous careers as a golf professional, golf course manager, and more recently as Senior Estates Worker for a wildlife trust, have allowed me to enjoy my time in roles of both a physical and corporate nature. I have been fortunate to acquire, along the way, a wide-ranging and varied skill set in both management and practical situations.
With experience working alone and as part of a team, my past roles have generally culminated in a senior position involving mentoring and developing newer team members and trainees, which I have always found rewarding and fulfilling.

And now!..

My new journey begins...! I would truly love the next stage of my career to be within an environment which is stimulating, creative and allows for continual progress in order to keep the career path fresh and exciting.
Software development seems to tick all those boxes and I am pleased to be embarking on this new dynamic adventure.
The iO Academy bootcamp has been a great vehicle with which to start this process and has reaffirmed the many and varied opportunities that this career can offer. The content of the course has been comprehensive and the challenges have fitted well with my thirst for knowledge and my lifelong interest for problem and puzzle solving. I am excited at the prospect of taking the next steps to increase my skills and apply them to real world projects.

My interests....

As mentioned above, I enjoy keeping an active mind with quizzes, cryptic and other crosswords, anagrams and general word games. I have also enjoyed creating a number of themed anagram challenges which I have used to raise money for local charities.
In addition, as a keen bird watcher from the age of about 8 years old I have developed a life-long love of nature and wildlife. A planned trip to South Africa in 2005 prompted me to take up photography, a hobby which has grown to a passion over the years, during which I have acquired an L.R.P.S distinction with the Royal Photographic Society and had some success in competitions in the advanced section of a local camera club.
Venturing into the realms of macro photography in recent years has opened my eyes to a whole new world of miniature wonder and stimulated a deep interest in invertebrates and other tiny life forms which go largely unnoticed. Following this mini safari, with all the symbiosis and daily 'life and death' struggles encapsulates my thirst for knowledge.
